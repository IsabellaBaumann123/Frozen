In the second Frozen movie, Elsa hears a mysterious voice calling out to her, which leads her on a journey with her sister Anna, Kristoff, Sven, and Olaf to an enchanted forest. They encounter new characters and face dangerous challenges as they try to uncover the secrets of the enchanted forest. Ultimately, Elsa saves the day by sacrificing herself and entrusting Anna with the knowledge and responsibility to lead their kingdom towards a new future. The movie explores themes of identity, responsibility, and the power of sisterly love.

Anna and Kristoff have a developing romantic relationship in the Frozen franchise. They meet during Anna's journey to find Elsa in the first movie, and Kristoff helps her on her quest. As they travel together, they start to develop feelings for each other. In the second movie, Kristoff plans to propose to Anna, but various mishaps and interruptions get in the way. While their love story is not the primary focus of the movies, it adds to the overall theme of the importance of love and relationships in one's life.

The parents of Anna and Elsa play a significant role in the Frozen franchise. They are introduced in flashbacks in the first movie, where their tragic death prompts Elsa to become queen and leads to the events of the movie. In the second movie, their past is explored further, revealing their connection to the enchanted forest and the origin of Elsa's powers. The parents are shown to have had good intentions but made mistakes, such as hiding Elsa away and erasing Anna's memories. Their legacy serves as a reminder of the importance of communication and trust in familial relationships.
